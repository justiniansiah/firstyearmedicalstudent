No one blames you for the losses; after all, everyone is doing their best, and you are but a medical student. Blame or not, though, there seems to be a pall over Nigirite General: where people used to appear grimly determined in their tasks, they are now merely grim.

Upon consulting the duty rosters, you see why: Nigirite General hardly has enough people left to keep operations up. Enough, but barely so. It will work out, you tell yourself, adversity brings out the best in us. And, with everybody doing their best, things will work out.

Life, unfortunately, is not so kind.

Hope is a powerful resource, but with the recent spate of deaths, it has run dry.

It began with a batch of vaccines disappearing, then some rations. You turn a blind eye, determined to ensure that in the end you can face failure with a clear conscience.

Things, however, truly escalate when a group of nurses can no longer be found. Their quarters are found to be stripped bare, and an entire carton of can food is missing from the stores.

You and the other medical staff try your best to quash the news, but somehow it spreads like wildfire throughout Nigirite. That very day, the remaining policemen grab whatever they can, and walk out in full view of the packed hospital lobby.

The esteemed Dr. Johnson steps up to address the crowd, and for a moment it seems that his sonorous baritone might just be what Nigirite needed. Then an angry cry rises from the crowd, and the spell is broken, order is lost. The very people who once pitifully looked to you to save them now appear threatening, dangerous.
